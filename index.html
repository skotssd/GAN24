Registration now CLOSED for the 35th edition of the Gananoque Conference of Environmental Science and Engineering.

The Gananoque conference is a student-based initiative to gather those who are currently conducting research in environmental science for their graduate thesis or undergraduate honours project. The conference provides an opportunity for students to practice their presentation skills, present their research in front of a larger audience, and to meet fellow peers to discuss their research in a relaxed environment. Students are invited to share preliminary results, experimental work plans, as well as more refined results and findings.

Instructions

To participate  you will need to register with the hotel for accomodations (includes meals) or food-only packages.  Please see the "Accomodations" tab for details.  You will ALSO need to register for the conference with the academic team (see the "Registration" tab).  You need to do this even if you are not presenting.  

Some history behind the congress

The Gananoque conference was first organised in 1984 by INRS-ETE (University of Quebec) and McMaster University. Gananoque, ON was chosen as the approximate central location between Hamilton and Quebec City. Over the intervening years, presentations have covered areas of environmental science, including aquatic sciences, biogeochemistry, geology, environmental engineering, and toxicology. Students from many universities, including McMaster, INRS-ETE, Laurentian, Laurier, Trent, Toronto, Clarkson, Windsor, Queens, Ottawa and McGill, have regularly attended the conference.
